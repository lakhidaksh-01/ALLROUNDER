WORKING


The solar-powered USB power bank utilizes a solar panel (6V-180mA) to convert sunlight into electrical energy. A charge controller regulates this energy, preventing overcharging and ensuring safe battery charging. The stored energy in the internal Rechargeable LITHIUM-ION 3.4 2000mAh battery can then be used to charge devices via USB output. The system offers a convenient and eco-friendly way to harness renewable energy for on-the-go device charging. The energy can be also use for the torch and other features in the devices.

In this Bluetooth speaker feature, a Bluetooth module establishes a wireless connection with a source device, receiving audio signals. These signals are then amplified by a dedicated amplifier to a level suitable for the connected speaker. The speaker, in turn, converts the amplified signals into audible sound waves. The entire system is powered by a stable power supply. User controls, such as volume and power buttons, may be incorporated for ease of use and functionality.

The device includes a microphone to capture sound, a transmitter to modulate the signal onto a radio frequency carrier wave, and an antenna to broadcast the signal. The transmitted radio signal travels through the air to a receiving two-way radio, where a receiver demodulates the signal. The demodulated audio is then played through a speaker. The entire circuit is powered by batteries. It ranges up to 150 feet which is installed for disabled people to communicate in their house and can work for lazy people too.

Q5. Explain the stepwise working of the innovation explaining all the components used in the project and the specific function they are performing.


  1. Solar Panels (2) (6V, 0.36A / 2.16 WATTS):
    The solar panels harness sunlight and convert it into electrical energy.


  2. Volt Regulator 5V:
    The voltage regulator ensures that the output voltage remains stable at 5V, regardless of fluctuations in input voltage from the solar panels.


  3. USB Power Bank with LED Indicators of Charging, Rechargeable Lithium-Ion Battery:
    The USB power bank serves as a backup power source, storing energy from the solar panel.
    The rechargeable lithium-ion battery stores the energy for later use.


  4. Switches (3):
    The switches control various functions of the circuit, such as turning on/off the Bluetooth music system, torch, and wireless communication.


  5. Bluetooth Amplifier, 2 Speakers (10W):
    The Bluetooth amplifier receives audio signals from paired devices via Bluetooth and amplifies them.
    The speakers produce sound output from the amplified audio signals, providing high-quality music playback.


  6. Speaker for Wireless Communication (3W), Audio Amplifier for Wireless Communication:
    The speaker dedicated to wireless communication broadcasts audio signals transmitted wirelessly.
    The audio amplifier boosts the strength of the audio signals for clear and audible communication.


  7. Two-Way Radio PCB Board of 5V:
    The two-way radio PCB board facilitates wireless communication, allowing the circuit to communicate with other devices up to 150 feet away.


  8. Audio Jack, Antenna:
    The audio jack serves as a connection point for external audio devices.
    The antenna is used for transmitting and receiving wireless communication signals over a long range.



Stepwise Working:


1. The solar panels absorb sunlight and convert it into electrical energy, which is regulated to a stable 5V output by the voltage regulator.

2. The regulated power is then used to charge the rechargeable lithium-ion battery via the USB power bank.

3. The switches control the activation of different features such as Bluetooth music system, torch, and wireless communication.

4. When the Bluetooth music system is activated, audio signals from paired devices are received by the Bluetooth amplifier and played through the speakers.

5. Similarly, when the wireless communication feature is activated, audio signals are transmitted and received via the dedicated speaker and audio amplifier, respectively, using the two-way radio PCB board and antenna.

6. Users can enjoy wireless communication, music playback, and use the torch as needed, all powered by solar energy and supplemented by the rechargeable battery when required.
